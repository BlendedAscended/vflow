/ 01 — MOTION PRIMITIVES

Nine ideas, worth stealing.

13g's craft is in restraint. Text doesn't fly — it slides one letter at a time. Logos don't pop — they drift. Hover states aren't scale 1.05 — they're weight shifts. Here's each primitive, live.

01 · Kinetic Swap

Headline word rotator

A masked vertical crossfade that swaps the final word of a headline every ~1.7s. On 13g it's how they say "Réveillez votre marque / croissance / stratégie" in one breath.

Apply to HeroSection.tsx — rotate the final word of your H1 between "best customers", "high-intent leads", "loyal advocates". Positions Verbaflow as breadth-of-outcome, not one feature.

CSS ~40 lines · no JS
Timing 5s loop, ease-out-expo
Risk Low — contained to 1 word
02 · Type Reveal

Letter rise on enter

Each character sits below a mask and rises with a staggered delay (≈30ms). The effect reads as "typeset arriving" rather than "animation playing".

Apply to every H2 section title — "We work with winners", "Trusted by growth-minded businesses", pricing headers. Fires once on scroll-into-view via IntersectionObserver.

04 · On-Scroll Reveal

Fade + rise, cascaded

Items enter from +40px below, fading in with a per-item delay (80ms / 160ms / 240ms). The key is the easing — ease-out-expo — which slows dramatically in the last 20% of motion. Feels mechanical on ease-out; premium on ease-out-expo.

Replace your current animate-fade-in-up class. Apply to ServicesSection cards, PricingSection tiers, TestimonialsSection, FAQ rows. Replace ease-out everywhere with cubic-bezier(0.19, 1, 0.22, 1).

05 · Counter

Number ticks up on view

On scroll-in, numbers animate from 0 to their target over ~1.6s with ease-out. 13g uses this for "+130 clients", "+76 awards", "+10 years". It converts abstract stats into something you watch happen.

Add a stats band between HeroSection and ServicesSection: "+247 leads generated / 68% avg response rate / +1,204 tasks automated". Your glass card data becomes the hero proof point.

06 · Magnetic CTA

Button bends toward cursor

The button translates a small amount (~30% of cursor delta from center) as the cursor approaches. Combined with arrow-slide and glow-on-hover, it makes CTAs feel interactive before you click.

Apply only to your hero "Get my growth plan" button and the pricing CTAs. Don't magnetize every button — it loses meaning. Add arrow-slide (→ moves 6px right) to every vf-btn--primary.

08 · Ambient drift

Slow-drift radial gradients

Two oversized blurred radials drift independently over 14–18s. Your hero already has one; 13g uses two moving in opposition to create a subtle parallax without any imagery.

09 · Cursor Affordance

Contextual cursor dot

Over interactive zones, the native cursor vanishes and a branded dot with a verb ("View", "Play", "Drag") appears. Scales from 0 → 1 with ease-out-expo. Strongest on portfolio / case-study cards.

Use sparingly — only on the portfolio / case study grid (if you add one) and the Agency page. Don't ship globally; breaks accessibility and disorients form users.

/ 03 — BEYOND MOTION

Six other things 13g
does right.

Motion is half the story. These structural choices are why the site feels confident.

01
Oversized typography
13g's H1 is routinely 120–160px on desktop. Yours is clamp(44px, 5.2vw, 76px) — reasonable but safe. Push your hero to clamp(56px, 7vw, 110px) and cut sub-copy by 30%. Big type is the new whitespace.
02
One-word positioning
Their section labels are single verbs in caps: BRANDING · DIGITAL · CONTENT · STRATÉGIE. You have "Services", "About", "Blog". Rewrite service titles as single verbs: "Attract / Convert / Automate / Scale". Stronger scent of action.
03
Case-study-first thinking
Their homepage leads with work, not features. Three big case-study tiles above the fold (Adobe, Lemlist, CMA CGM). You lead with a pricing-adjacent growth plan. Consider: add a "Recent wins" strip — 3 client logos with 1-line result ("+40% bookings for [client]") before pricing.
04
Numbered sections
Every section has a tiny mono-font counter — / 01, / 02, / 03 — in the corner. Free structure, feels editorial, earns the premium positioning. Cheap to ship; adopt immediately. (This doc uses it.)
05
Asymmetric grids
13g almost never uses symmetric layouts. Hero text is left-aligned with content at 60% width; case studies are 70/30 or 40/60 splits; image grids have one oversized hero + two small satellites. Your current 2×2 and 3×3 grids read as a template. Break one grid per page to regain craft.
06
Quiet color palette
Their palette is 95% near-black + one near-white, with accent color used maybe 3 times per page. You use accent green liberally (button, badge, glow, border, text-2). Try a rule: accent only on CTAs and one "key number" per section. The restraint will make the accent hit harder.
